Understanding Calories

What Are Calories?

A calorie is a unit of energy. Scientifically, it’s the amount of energy needed to raise the temperature of 1 gram of water by 1°C. In nutrition, calories come from food and fuel your body’s functions—breathing, digestion, and movement (source: WHO).

Your Basal Metabolic Rate (BMR) accounts for 60-70% of daily calorie use, with the rest from physical activity and digestion (source: NIH).

Managing Calories

Your weight depends on calorie balance:

  • Deficit: Burn more than you eat to lose weight (e.g., 500-calorie deficit = ~0.5 lb loss/week).
  • Surplus: Eat more than you burn to gain weight.
  • Maintenance: Match intake to expenditure.
